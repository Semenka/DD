Exec summary

Revoy is a modular truck electrification bet, not a full EV tractor bet, with a fifth-wheel dolly that claims 235 electric miles and under-five-minute swaps [37].

The investable inflection is diesel-lane fuel savings before OEM BEV replacement, supported by renewed U.S. attention to truck battery swapping [14].

The deal is unpriceable today because Revoy asks for $50.0M at a $200.0M valuation with no disclosed ARR, MRR, growth, margin, customer count, retention, or revenue quality. (from DealContext)

The founder section is blank and the co-investor section failed, so Principle 3 and Principle 8 diligence are incomplete. (from Founders) (from Co-investors)

Market

Inflection thesis

The inflection is modular truck electrification for diesel lanes, not full EV truck replacement. Revoy claims a fifth-wheel dolly with a 525 kWh LFP pack can lift diesel tractor efficiency from 6-8 MPG to 20-35+ MPG, swap in under five minutes, and run 235 electric miles without truck or trailer modifications [37]. Battery swapping is getting renewed U.S. attention because cable charging creates downtime and battery-as-a-service can cut upfront HDV cost by up to 50% [14].

Sizing

  • Today: The closest direct proxy is global heavy-duty electric trucks at $1.93B in 2025; North America was $47.4M in 2025 [40] [41]. Analyst definitions conflict, with GMI sizing electric heavy-duty trucks at $16.97B in 2025 and broader electric-truck sources at $18.50B in 2025 [42] [43].
  • Five-year: The direct proxy implies $3.86B in 2030 if Grand View’s 15.1% CAGR compounds from its 2025 base (calculation from [40]). Broader electric truck forecasts reach $58.50B by 2030, while electric commercial vehicles reach $255.6B by 2030 when pickups, vans, buses, and medium-duty trucks are included [43] [44].
  • Wedge to serve: The first wedge is North American Class 8 hub-to-hub lanes where route repetition supports swap-station utilization and fleets avoid new tractor capex [14]. ATRI puts 2024 fuel cost at $0.481/mile across for-hire carriers, which is the budget line Revoy attacks before full tractor replacement [56].
