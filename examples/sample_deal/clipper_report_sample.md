## Investment Memo (long-form, Bessemer-style)

### Investment Thesis
Pitch is building collaborative presentation software at the moment AI reopens the deck-creation workflow [2]. The asymmetric case is that decks stop being static files and become a multiplayer revenue workflow for sales, marketing, and executive teams (speculation). The starting point is thin: $3.2M ARR, 12 paying customers, and Wayfair named as a customer (from memo).

The problem is not only traction. The source package mixes Pitch.com with a warehouse bin-picking robotics memo [2]. That makes the underwriting file unreliable before the valuation question begins. At a $100M ask, Pitch is priced at 31.3x ARR while growth, retention, gross margin, burn, Magic Number, and customer concentration are undisclosed (from memo). The reverse DCF requires 58% annual revenue growth for 7 years to reach $77.3M terminal revenue at a 25% terminal FCF margin (from reverse DCF).

### Company
Pitch appears to be a presentation software company focused on collaborative deck creation, workflows, design, and personalized pitch rooms [2] [50] [51]. The company sits in a market defined by PowerPoint, Google Slides, Canva, Figma-style collaboration patterns, and sales-content workflows (speculation). The product case is that teams want multiplayer deck creation instead of sending files around (speculation).

The founder story is missing from the extracted file. No founder names, LinkedIn URLs, shipped artifacts, prior companies, public writing, or product cadence were available in the inputs. That is a material gap because this category depends on founder taste, product velocity, and distribution judgment.

> I would not let the clean product narrative outrun the broken file. The first diligence step is confirming the company, founders, and cap table.

### Market
If the target is Pitch, the investable market is presentation creation and team content workflows. G2 describes Pitch as collaborative presentation software for teams, and Capterra describes it as presentation software focused on real-time collaboration, workflows, and design features [48] [51]. The wedge is narrow: replace static deck creation with collaborative, AI-assisted presentation workflows inside teams (speculation).

If the target is the robotics company described in the market section, the market is warehouse robotics, not presentation software. Analyst estimates place warehouse robotics at $4.93B in 2023, $7.1B in 2024, and $7.35B in 2026 [49] [50] [51]. The cited drivers are e-commerce, labor shortages, AMRs, AGVs, AI, ML-enabled picking, and sorting [48] [51]. That is a real market, but it is not Pitch.

The core market issue is identity integrity. Kiva Systems is the winner pattern after Amazon acquired it for $775M [63]. Berkshire Grey is the warning pattern after its $375M go-private transaction [62]. Prezi is the product-love failure analog for Pitch because presentation usage does not automatically become budget-critical (speculation).

### Why Now
The Pitch version of why-now is that AI makes presentation generation faster and collaborative content workflows more fluid [2]. That could reopen a mature category controlled by Microsoft PowerPoint, Google Slides, and Canva (speculation). The robotics version of why-now is AI perception plus robotic control improving warehouse picking [47] [48]. The file cannot support both why-now stories at once.

### Team

No shipped artifacts, prior product history, public writing, hiring signals, or founder-market fit evidence were available. This removes the highest-signal diligence input for an early-stage product bet.

> I would treat the missing founder file as a stop sign, not an admin issue. In this category, founder taste and cadence are the company.
