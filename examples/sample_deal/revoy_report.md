## Market

### Inflection thesis

The inflection is modular truck electrification for diesel lanes, not full EV truck replacement. Revoy claims a fifth-wheel dolly with a 525 kWh LFP pack can lift diesel tractor efficiency from 6-8 MPG to 20-35+ MPG, swap in under five minutes, and run 235 electric miles without truck or trailer modifications [[37](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/revoy-launches-the-ultimate-scalable-sustainability-solution-for-trucking-302049374.html)]. Battery swapping is getting renewed U.S. attention because cable charging creates downtime and battery-as-a-service can cut upfront HDV cost by up to 50% [[14](https://www.aceee.org/sites/default/files/pdfs/battery_swapping_for_truck_electrification_in_the_united_states.pdf)].

### Sizing

- **Today**: The closest direct proxy is global heavy-duty electric trucks at $1.93B in 2025; North America was $47.4M in 2025 [[40](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/heavy-duty-electric-trucks-market-report)] [[41](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/horizon/outlook/heavy-duty-electric-trucks-market/north-america)]. Analyst definitions conflict, with GMI sizing electric heavy-duty trucks at $16.97B in 2025 and broader electric-truck sources at $18.50B in 2025 [[42](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/electric-heavy-duty-trucks-market)] [[43](https://marqstats.com/reports/electric-truck-market/)].
- **Five-year**: The direct proxy implies $3.86B in 2030 if Grand View’s 15.1% CAGR compounds from its 2025 base (calculation from [[40](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/heavy-duty-electric-trucks-market-report)]). Broader electric truck forecasts reach $58.50B by 2030, while electric commercial vehicles reach $255.6B by 2030 when pickups, vans, buses, and medium-duty trucks are included [[43](https://marqstats.com/reports/electric-truck-market/)] [[44](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/03/19/2848667/0/en/Electric-Commercial-Vehicle-Market-worth-255-6-billion-by-2030-Exclusive-Report-by-MarketsandMarkets.html)].
- **Wedge to serve**: The first wedge is North American Class 8 hub-to-hub lanes where route repetition supports swap-station utilization and fleets avoid new tractor capex [[14](https://www.aceee.org/sites/default/files/pdfs/battery_swapping_for_truck_electrification_in_the_united_states.pdf)]. ATRI puts 2024 fuel cost at $0.481/mile across for-hire carriers, which is the budget line Revoy attacks before full tractor replacement [[56](https://trid.trb.org/View/2570650)].
