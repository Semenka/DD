# Market timing and inflection points

Markets do not grow at constant rates. They have inflection points. The job of the investor — and of the founder — is to identify which markets are at an inflection point and which are mature.

Inflections come from a small list of causes:

- A new platform that changes the cost structure (mobile, cloud, LLMs).
- A new buyer behavior that changes the distribution model (consumerization of enterprise, prosumer SaaS, AI-driven outbound).
- A regulatory unlock or shock (HIPAA changes, GDPR, financial deregulation, cannabis legalization).
- A sub-cost frontier crossed (sequencing, batteries, compute, robotics).
- A demographic shift big enough to change demand (aging, climate migration, China decoupling).

Saying "this market is large" is not interesting. Saying "this market is large AND there is a specific change happening this year that has not been priced in by incumbents" is interesting.

Crucially, inflections are visible to many people. Your edge as an investor or a founder is not seeing the inflection. It is identifying the specific wedge inside the inflection — the narrow first product, the precise customer segment, the exact go-to-market motion that wins the early share.

Snowflake was not the first cloud data warehouse. They were the right wedge — separation of storage and compute, with a developer-first onboarding, at the moment enterprises were giving up on Teradata. Cloudera saw the same inflection earlier and won a smaller version of the prize.

The right question is never "is this market big". The right question is "what specifically is changing right now, and does this company own the precise wedge that captures the first 1 billion of the resulting opportunity".
