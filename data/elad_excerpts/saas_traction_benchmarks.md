# SaaS traction — Rule of 40, Magic Number, growth percentiles

The standard public-SaaS benchmarks are well-known but consistently misused at the private-company stage.

**Rule of 40.** Annual growth rate + free cash flow margin ≥ 40. At Series B and beyond this is the single most reliable signal of durability. Companies above Rule of 40 trade at meaningful premiums to companies below. The top quartile of public SaaS is consistently above 60.

**Magic Number.** (Net new ARR in a quarter, annualized) divided by (sales + marketing spend in the prior quarter). > 1.0 is healthy at scale. < 0.5 is usually a sign that growth is being bought rather than earned. Be careful with early-stage Magic Number — at sub-10 million ARR the denominator is noisy and the ratio swings wildly.

**Net retention.** Above 110% net retention is the single most underrated signal. A company doing 100% growth with 110% net retention is much more valuable than a company doing 100% growth with 90% net retention. The first compounds. The second is a leaky bucket.

**Growth percentile.** Public SaaS distributions are public — pull the latest set of 30 names from a single source (e.g. Meritech, Bessemer) and compute the percentile rank for any private company you are evaluating. A 100% YoY grower at $10M ARR is at roughly the 90th percentile of the public distribution. A 40% grower at $50M ARR is at roughly the 50th.

The point is not to apply public multiples to private companies. The point is to know where on the distribution your target sits, and to price accordingly. If you are paying a 50x multiple on $10M ARR, you are implicitly betting the company is in the top 10% of the public distribution five years from now. Be honest about whether that is the bet.
